# Identity Theft Protection

Safeguard your personal information and prevent identity theft.

## What is Identity Theft?

Criminals steal personal information to commit fraud, open accounts, or make purchases in your name.

## Common Methods

- Data breaches
- Phishing emails
- Social engineering
- Mail theft
- Public Wi-Fi snooping
- Dumpster diving
- Card skimming

## Warning Signs

- Unexpected credit denials
- Unknown charges
- Missing mail
- Calls about unknown accounts
- IRS notice for unreported income
- Medical bills for services not received

## Prevention Steps

### Protect Personal Information
- Shred sensitive documents
- Use strong unique passwords
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Limit social media sharing
- Secure mail delivery
- Review privacy settings

### Monitor Your Credit
- Check credit reports annually (free)
- Sign up for credit monitoring
- Freeze credit if not needed
- Set up fraud alerts
- Review statements monthly

### Secure Your Devices
- Keep software updated
- Use antivirus protection
- Enable device encryption
- Lock screens automatically
- Use VPN on public Wi-Fi

## Credit Freeze vs Lock

**Freeze:**
- Free by law
- Blocks all access
- Requires PIN to lift
- Best for long-term

**Lock:**
- May have fees
- Instant on/off
- Online management
- Good for frequent changes

## If Your Identity is Stolen

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Place fraud alert with credit bureaus
2. File police report
3. Contact Federal Trade Commission (IdentityTheft.gov)
4. Notify banks and creditors
5. Change all passwords
6. Review credit reports

**Credit Bureaus:**
- Equifax: 1-800-525-6285
- Experian: 1-888-397-3742
- TransUnion: 1-800-680-7289

## Recovery Steps

1. Document everything
2. Close fraudulent accounts
3. Dispute unauthorized charges
4. File identity theft report
5. Get extended fraud alert (7 years)
6. Monitor for 1-2 years minimum

## Special Considerations

### Children's Identity
- Freeze minor's credit
- Monitor for activity
- Check before 18th birthday
- Limit SSN sharing

### Seniors
- More vulnerable to scams
- Designate trusted contact
- Regular account review
- Limit sharing information

### Tax Identity Theft
- File early
- Use IP PIN from IRS
- Monitor tax transcripts
- Report immediately

## Social Security Protection

- Memorize number
- Don't carry card
- Limit sharing
- Verify requesters
- Report lost/stolen immediately

## Medical Identity Theft

**Prevention:**
- Review Explanation of Benefits
- Check medical records
- Secure insurance cards
- Verify billing statements
- Request annual medical report

**If Compromised:**
- Contact providers
- Request corrections
- File police report
- Notify insurance
- Get treatment records
