# VPN Usage Guide

Understanding Virtual Private Networks and when to use them.

## What is a VPN?

A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet, hiding your IP address and protecting your data.

## Benefits of Using a VPN

- **Privacy** - Hide your browsing activity from ISP
- **Security** - Encrypt data on public Wi-Fi
- **Anonymity** - Mask your IP address
- **Access** - Bypass geo-restrictions
- **Protection** - Prevent tracking

## When to Use a VPN

- Public Wi-Fi networks (coffee shops, airports)
- Remote work connections
- Accessing blocked content
- Online banking on untrusted networks
- Protecting sensitive communications

## Choosing a VPN Provider

### Key Features
- No-logs policy
- Strong encryption (AES-256)
- Kill switch
- Multiple server locations
- Fast connection speeds
- Split tunneling

### Popular VPN Services
1. NordVPN
2. ExpressVPN
3. ProtonVPN
4. Surfshark
5. Mullvad

## VPN Limitations

- May slow connection speed
- Some websites block VPN traffic
- Not 100% anonymous
- Requires trust in VPN provider
- May cost monthly fees

## Setup Guide

1. Choose reputable VPN provider
2. Download and install app
3. Create account and login
4. Select server location
5. Connect to VPN
6. Verify connection (check IP)

## Best Practices

- Always use VPN on public Wi-Fi
- Choose nearby servers for speed
- Enable kill switch
- Verify no DNS leaks
- Use reputable paid services

## Free vs Paid VPNs

Free VPNs often have limitations: slower speeds, data caps, fewer servers, and may log your data. Paid VPNs offer better security and performance.
