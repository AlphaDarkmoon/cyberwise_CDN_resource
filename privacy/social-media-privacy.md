# Social Media Privacy Settings

Protect your personal information on social media platforms.

## Why Privacy Matters

- Prevent identity theft
- Reduce targeted advertising
- Control who sees your content
- Protect personal information
- Minimize digital footprint

## Facebook Privacy

### Key Settings
1. **Profile visibility** - Friends only
2. **Post audience** - Custom lists
3. **Tag review** - Approve tags before posting
4. **Face recognition** - Disable
5. **Location services** - Turn off
6. **App permissions** - Review and remove

### Steps to Secure
- Settings → Privacy
- Limit past posts
- Review tagged photos
- Control friend requests
- Disable public search

## Instagram Privacy

- Make account private
- Approve follower requests
- Hide story from specific people
- Disable activity status
- Remove followers
- Control comment filters

## Twitter/X Privacy

- Protect tweets (private account)
- Disable photo tagging
- Control who can tag you
- Limit location information
- Review connected apps
- Enable two-factor authentication

## LinkedIn Privacy

- Control profile visibility
- Limit connection suggestions
- Manage activity broadcasts
- Hide connections
- Control who can see your email
- Adjust search engine indexing

## General Best Practices

1. **Review privacy settings quarterly**
2. **Limit personal information**
3. **Think before posting**
4. **Use strong passwords**
5. **Enable 2FA on all accounts**
6. **Be cautious with friend requests**
7. **Review app permissions**

## What NOT to Share

- Full birthdate
- Home address
- Phone number
- Current location (real-time)
- Travel plans
- Financial information
- Children's information
- Passwords or security questions

## Privacy Checklist

✓ Profile set to private/friends only  
✓ Two-factor authentication enabled  
✓ Location services disabled  
✓ Tag review activated  
✓ Third-party apps reviewed  
✓ Search engine indexing limited  
✓ Activity status hidden  

Remember: Once online, always online. Think twice before posting anything.
