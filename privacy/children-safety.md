# Children's Online Safety

Protect children while navigating the digital world.

## Age-Appropriate Guidelines

**Ages 0-5:** Minimal screen time, supervised only
**Ages 6-9:** Educational content, strict parental controls
**Ages 10-12:** Limited social media, monitored usage
**Ages 13+:** Gradual independence, open communication

## Parental Control Tools

### Built-In Options
- **iOS:** Screen Time, App Limits
- **Android:** Family Link, Digital Wellbeing
- **Windows:** Family Safety
- **macOS:** Screen Time, Parental Controls

### Third-Party Solutions
- Qustodio
- Net Nanny
- Norton Family
- Bark (monitors content)
- Kaspersky Safe Kids

## Social Media Safety

### Rules to Establish
- Private accounts only
- Approve all followers
- No personal information shared
- No location tagging
- Think before posting
- Report bullying immediately

### Platforms with Age Restrictions
Most require 13+:
- Instagram
- TikTok
- Snapchat
- Twitter/X
- Facebook

## Gaming Safety

- Enable privacy settings
- Disable in-game purchases
- Use parental controls
- Monitor friend lists
- Understand rating systems (ESRB)
- Limit voice chat
- Set time limits

## Cyberbullying Prevention

**Warning Signs:**
- Withdrawal from activities
- Secretive behavior
- Mood changes
- Avoiding school/devices
- Sleep problems

**Action Steps:**
1. Document evidence
2. Report to platform
3. Contact school
4. Support your child
5. Consider counseling
6. Report to authorities if severe

## Privacy Education

Teach children:
- Never share passwords
- Don't talk to strangers online
- Verify friend requests
- Protect personal information
- Recognize scams
- Ask parents before downloading

## Screen Time Balance

**Recommendations:**
- Under 2 years: None
- 2-5 years: 1 hour/day
- 6-12 years: 1-2 hours/day
- 13+: Negotiate limits

**Tips:**
- No screens during meals
- Tech-free bedrooms
- Family media plan
- Lead by example
- Encourage outdoor play

Create a contract covering:
- Screen time limits
- Allowed websites/apps
- Privacy rules
